Netlist Generation from Hand-Drawn Circuits

It started with a simple frustration: drawing circuits on paper is easy, but turning those sketches into something a simulator like LTspice can actually run usually takes hours of re-drawing and manual wiring. I wanted to bridge that gap ‚Äî could a computer look at a messy sketch and generate the digital netlist automatically?

That question led me to design a computer-vision pipeline that could recognize components, trace wires, and emit a ready-to-use netlist. I combined OpenCV preprocessing (binarization, skeletonization), HOG + SVM classification for components, and a bit of graph theory to connect nodes correctly.

The result? A system that takes a scanned hand-drawn circuit and outputs a simulator-ready netlist with around 95% accuracy on benchmarks. No more re-drawing ‚Äî just sketch ‚Üí scan ‚Üí simulate.

What made this rewarding was how many skills came together: image processing cleaned noisy sketches, machine learning gave the system ‚Äúeyes‚Äù to classify components, and netlist logic stitched it all into a real, testable circuit.

Highlights

  • Preprocessing pipeline: denoising, adaptive thresholding, skeletonization.
  • HOG + SVM classifier to identify resistors, diodes, op-amps, and inductors.
  • Rule-based detection for capacitors, grounds, and voltage sources.
  • Graph-based node and wire tracing to ensure valid connectivity.
  • Outputs LTspice-compatible netlists from scanned circuit images.
